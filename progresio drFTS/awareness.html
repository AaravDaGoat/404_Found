Waterborne Diseases Awareness

Severity of Waterborne Diseases

Waterborne infections are one of humanity's most persistent and economically destructive biological threats. In impoverished nations, waterborne diseases cause four-fifths of all illnesses, with diarrhea being the leading cause of pediatric mortality. Each year, an estimated 1.8 million people are killed by waterborne illnesses such as cholera, typhoid, urinary tract infections, schistosomiasis, and other diarrheal diseases. Despite this, waterborne diarrhea remains a prominent cause of mortality and disease among children in developing countries, accounting for 90% of diarrhea deaths in children under the age of five.

Common Symptoms

Common symptoms include diarrhea, vomiting, nausea, stomach cramps, fever, skin, ear or eye problems, cough, and shortness of breath.

Prevention

To prevent such diseases from occurring, we can take certain precautions which will help provide a safer environment free of waterborne diseases. These include washing fruits and vegetables well, washing hands often, and drinking safe water. To treat the diseases, drink plenty of safe water, rest, and taking over-the-counter diarrheal medication is important.
